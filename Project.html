Automated Classroom System - Electronic Sensors

In my first-year academics, I developed a smart classroom system aimed at promoting energy conservation through automation. The primary motivation behind this project was to address the common issue of lights and fans being left on in school classrooms after school, leading to unnecessary electricity wastage. To tackle this, I designed a system that could intelligently control electrical appliances based on environmental conditions and room occupancy.

The system was built using an Arduino microcontroller and integrated with various sensors. An LDR (Light Dependent Resistor) was used to detect ambient light levels, enabling the system to automatically turn off lights during the day and switch them on when it gets dark. A thermistor was employed to monitor temperature changes, activating the fans only when the classroom became warm. To determine whether the classroom was in use, I used a reed switch placed on the door; when the door was closed and no activity was detected, the system assumed the room was unoccupied and turned off all appliances. The control of AC-powered devices like lights and fans was managed using relay modules, which acted as switches between the microcontroller and the high-voltage components.

  • Automated Light ON/OFF System : Identify the day, night and the dark light condition like rainy day.
  • Automated Fan ON/OFF System : Identify the hot and cold of the environment.
  • Classroom Door Close Identification : Controlling of the circuit currents and voltage.
